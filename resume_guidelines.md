# Resume Tips

## Exporting

- **Canva, Google Docs** — export as PDF, nothing special needed
- **Word** — Save as PDF → pick *"Best for electronic distribution"*, not "Minimum size"
- **Overleaf** — if text comes out garbled, switch to XeLaTeX (Menu → Compiler)

## Sections

- Keep certifications and hackathon wins separate — recruiters treat them differently
- "Skills & Interests" combined means your interests get mixed in with your tech skills
- Leadership and extracurriculars are worth their own section if you have them

## Projects

Two things make the biggest difference:

- **List your tech stack on its own line** below the project name — don't bury it in the description
- **Add numbers** — accuracy, latency, scale, users. "96% accuracy on 10K samples" > "trained a model"

## Skills

List what you actually know. A short honest list beats a long padded one.
Use `Category: item1, item2` if you have groupings — it's cleaner for both humans and tools.

## General

- Name first line, contact second — sounds obvious but often gets buried in header graphics
- Add GitHub and LinkedIn if you have them
- One page up to ~2 years experience, two pages after
- Avoid tables for layout — they break in most resume screening tools
